Supervised learning is a type of machine learning algorithm that involves training a model on a labeled dataset. In supervised learning, the algorithm learns from examples that are already labeled by humans. The labeled data is used as a guide for the model to make predictions or decisions when new, unlabeled data is presented. 

Supervised learning can be divided into two main categories: classification and regression. In classification, the model is trained to assign a label to a data point based on its features. For example, a spam filter can be trained to classify emails as either spam or not spam based on features such as the sender, subject line, and content of the email. In regression, the model predicts a continuous value based on the input features. For example, a regression model can be trained to predict the price of a house based on its size, location, and other features.

Supervised learning is widely used in various industries, such as finance, healthcare, and marketing, to make informed decisions and predictions based on historical data. It is a powerful tool for pattern recognition, classification, and making predictions in a wide range of applications.
