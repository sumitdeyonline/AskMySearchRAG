Neural networks are a type of machine learning algorithm that is inspired by the way the human brain processes information. They consist of interconnected nodes, or neurons, that work together to process and analyze complex data sets. These networks are capable of learning and adapting to new information, making them incredibly powerful tools for tasks such as image recognition, speech recognition, and natural language processing.

Neural networks are structured in layers, with each layer containing a set of neurons that are interconnected with the neurons in the previous and next layers. The first layer is the input layer, which receives the raw data, such as an image or a piece of text. The last layer is the output layer, which produces the final result, such as a classification or prediction. In between the input and output layers are one or more hidden layers, which are responsible for extracting and learning patterns from the data.

During the training process, the neural network adjusts its connections based on the error between the predicted output and the actual output. This process is repeated many times until the network can accurately make predictions on new, unseen data. Once trained, neural networks are capable of processing large volumes of data quickly and efficiently, making them invaluable tools for a wide range of applications in fields such as healthcare, finance, and automotive industries.
