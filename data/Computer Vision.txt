Computer vision is a field of artificial intelligence that enables computers to interpret and understand visual information from the real world. It involves developing algorithms and techniques to help computers analyze and process images and videos in order to extract meaningful data and make decisions based on that data.

One of the key goals of computer vision is to enable machines to perceive and interpret the visual world in a similar way to humans. This involves tasks such as object recognition, object detection, image segmentation, and facial recognition.

Computer vision has a wide range of practical applications in various industries such as healthcare, automotive, security, retail, and entertainment. For example, in healthcare, computer vision can be used to analyze medical images such as X-rays and MRIs to assist radiologists in diagnosing diseases. In the automotive industry, computer vision is used in self-driving cars to help them navigate and detect obstacles on the road. In retail, computer vision can be used to track inventory and analyze customer behavior in stores.

Overall, computer vision plays a crucial role in enabling machines to interact with the visual world, making it a fundamental technology in the development of intelligent systems.
